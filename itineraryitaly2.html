Florence, Siena, Pisa & San Gimignano

Lose yourself in the birthplace of the Renaissance on this immersive guided tour through Tuscany's most extraordinary cities and landscapes. From the world-class art and architecture of Florence to the medieval pageantry of Siena, the iconic towers of Pisa and the timeless hilltop streets of San Gimignano, this journey weaves together masterpiece art, ancient history and the unhurried beauty of the Tuscan countryside — all in a single unforgettable nine-day experience.

Your journey begins in Florence, where the Renaissance was born and where its greatest masterpieces still stand. Over three days your expert art historian guide will lead you through the Uffizi Gallery — home to Botticelli's Birth of Venus and dozens of the world's most celebrated paintings — before taking you face to face with Michelangelo's David in the Galleria dell'Accademia. Wander across the ancient Ponte Vecchio, explore the vast Piazza della Signoria and climb to the top of Brunelleschi's magnificent Duomo for sweeping rooftop views over the city. Evenings are yours to discover the artisan workshops and candlelit restaurants of the Oltrarno district.

  • Guided tour of the Uffizi Gallery — Botticelli, Leonardo and Raphael
  • Stand before Michelangelo's David in the Galleria dell'Accademia
  • Climb Brunelleschi's Duomo — panoramic views over Florence's rooftops
  • Explore the Ponte Vecchio and Piazza della Signoria
  • Free evening in Oltrarno — artisan quarter, local trattorias and hidden piazzas
  • Optional: Boboli Gardens and Pitti Palace

Transfer into the heart of Tuscany to the great medieval city of Siena — Florence's ancient rival and one of Italy's best-preserved Gothic cities. Your guide will walk you through the extraordinary shell-shaped Piazza del Campo, into the soaring black-and-white striped Duomo and up through the winding medieval lanes of the city's ancient contrade neighbourhoods. A day excursion takes you to San Gimignano — a perfectly intact hilltop town whose fourteen surviving medieval towers rise dramatically above the Tuscan plain. Wind back through the Chianti wine country for a private tasting at a family-run vineyard amid olive groves and cypress trees.

  • Guided tour of Siena's Piazza del Campo — one of Europe's greatest medieval squares
  • Explore Siena Cathedral — stunning Gothic architecture and Pisano pulpit
  • Walk the medieval streets of San Gimignano — the Manhattan of the Middle Ages
  • Private Chianti wine tasting at a family estate amid rolling Tuscan vineyards
  • Panoramic drive through the Val d'Orcia — cypress-lined roads and hilltop villages
  • Optional: Montalcino — hilltop fortress town and home of Brunello wine

Your final stage takes you west to Pisa — a city far richer in history than its famous leaning tower alone suggests. The Campo dei Miracoli is one of the most breathtaking architectural ensembles in the world: the Tower, the vast marble Cathedral, the circular Baptistery and the hauntingly beautiful Camposanto all stand together on a sweep of emerald lawn. Your guide willbring the full story of this once-mighty maritime republic to life before you climb the tower's famous tilting spiral staircase for views across the Arno plain. A final free afternoon gives you time to explore Pisa's quieter riverside quarter, its bustling market streets and the elegant Piazza dei Cavalieri before your farewell dinner.

  • Climb the Leaning Tower of Pisa — iconic views from the tilting summit
  • Guided tour of the Campo dei Miracoli — Cathedral, Baptistery and Camposanto
  • Explore Piazza dei Cavalieri — Pisa's historic knight's square
  • Walk the Lungarni — Pisa's elegant riverside promenades
  • Free afternoon in Pisa — markets, cafés and the quieter medieval quarter
  • Farewell dinner before return transfer to Pisa International Airport
