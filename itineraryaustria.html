Vienna, Salzburg and Venice

Immerse yourself in the golden age of European music and architecture on this extraordinary journey from Mozart's Salzburg to Vivaldi's Venice. Hear the world's finest orchestras in their original Baroque concert halls, wander gilded palaces where emperors once held court, and glide through Venice's ancient canals to candlelit performances in churches where the masters themselves once played. This is Europe at its most opulent — where the music still echoes through the stone.

Your musical journey begins in Mozart's birthplace, Salzburg — a UNESCO-listed Baroque jewel nestled beneath the Alps. Your musicologist guide leads you through the composer's childhood home, the resonant halls of the Hohensalzburg Fortress, and the gold-leafed churches where his earliest works premiered. A private chamber music concert recreates the sound of Mozart's era, while evenings give you time to wander Mirabell Gardens and the candlelit Getreidegasse.

  • Private tour of Mozart's Geburtshaus — his childhood home and violin collection
  • Guided visit to Hohensalzburg Fortress — Europe's best-preserved medieval castle
  • Private chamber music concert in a Baroque hall with period instruments
  • Explore Mirabell Palace Gardens — Sound of Music locations and Baroque fountains
  • Free evening in the Altstadt — candlelit cafés and classical music bars

The imperial heart of European music awaits in Vienna. Your guide reveals the Hofburg Palace where emperors commissioned Haydn and Beethoven, the gilded halls of the Musikverein where the Vienna Philharmonic perfects their legendary acoustics, and Schönbrunn Palace where Mozart performed as a child prodigy. A world-class orchestra concert lets you hear the music as emperors once did, while coffee house afternoons offer time to absorb the city's operatic soul.

  • Private tour of Hofburg Palace — Imperial Apartments and Spanish Riding School
  • Guided visit to Musikverein Golden Hall — home of Vienna Philharmonic acoustics
  • Orchestra concert — Vienna State Opera or Musikverein (subject to availability)
  • Explore Schönbrunn Palace — where Mozart performed aged 6
  • Free afternoon in Vienna's coffee houses — literary cafés where Strauss composed

Your final destination is Vivaldi's Venice — the city of opulent churches, candlelit concerts, and labyrinthine canals. Glide by private gondola past palazzos where Casanova gambled and Goldoni wrote, attend a masked Vivaldi concert in the church where he composed his Four Seasons, and explore the echoing stone interiors of Santa Maria della Salute. This is Baroque Venice at its most seductive.

  • Private gondola ride at dusk — ancient canals, palazzos, hidden churches
  • Masked Vivaldi concert — Four Seasons in candlelit 17th-century church
  • Guided tour of Santa Maria della Salute — Baroque masterpiece by Longhena
  • Visit Scuola Grande di San Rocco — Tintoretto's Biblical cycle
  • Farewell dinner overlooking the Grand Canal before transfer to Venice Airport
