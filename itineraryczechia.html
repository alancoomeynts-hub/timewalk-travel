Prague, Karlštejn & Český Krumlov

Discover the architectural crown jewels of Bohemia — from Prague's golden spires and the world's largest castle complex, to Gothic fortresses guarding imperial treasures, and the fairy-tale town of Český Krumlov tumbling down a river bend. This is Central Europe at its most romantic, where emperors built to touch heaven and Renaissance nobles created perfect worlds in miniature.

Your Bohemian adventure begins in Prague — the city of a hundred spires. Your art historian guide reveals Prague Castle's vast courtyards where emperors ruled, the kaleidoscopic stained glass of St. Vitus Cathedral, and the ancient stones of Charles Bridge where alchemists once worked. A private classical concert in Dvořák's Rudolfinum brings the city's musical soul to life.

  • Private tour of Prague Castle — world's largest ancient castle complex
  • Guided visit to St. Vitus Cathedral — Gothic masterpiece with Mucha windows
  • Cross Charles Bridge at dawn — statues, towers, no crowds
  • Private classical concert — Rudolfinum Hall (Dvořák Philharmonic)
  • Explore Old Town Square — Astronomical Clock, Týn Church

A 40-minute private transfer southwest takes you to Karlštejn Castle — Charles IV's Gothic masterpiece perched above the Bohemian hills. Built to house the crown jewels and holy relics, this is the quintessential fairy-tale fortress with drawbridge, battlements, and frescoed chapels glittering with gold leaf.

  • Private guided tour of Karlštejn Castle — Imperial apartments and Holy Chapel
  • Explore the Great Tower — panoramic views over Berounka Valley
  • Time to wander Karlštejn village — medieval taverns and artisan shops
  • Return to Prague by late afternoon

Your private coach journeys 3 hours south to Český Krumlov — Europe's most perfectly preserved Renaissance town tumbling down a river bend. This UNESCO jewel features a mountaintop castle with Baroque theatre, pastel townhouses hugging the Vltava, and cobblestone streets where time stopped 400 years ago.

  • Private tour of Český Krumlov Castle — Europe's best Baroque theatre
  • Explore the town square — Renaissance facades, frescoed churches
  • Lunch overlooking the Vltava River bend
  • Optional raft trip through the town (seasonal)
  • Return to Prague by evening

Your final days return to Prague for its haunting Jewish heritage and literary cafés. Farewell dinner celebrates Bohemia's architectural wonders.

  • Private tour of Jewish Quarter — Old Cemetery, Pinkas Synagogue
  • Free time in Malá Strana — Baroque palaces, hidden gardens
  • Farewell dinner at your 5-star hotel
  • Transfer to Prague Airport
