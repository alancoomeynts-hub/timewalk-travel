Your guided holiday to Ireland & Scotland

Journey from Cork's medieval Blarney Castle through Ireland's ancient heartland to Belfast's Titanic shipyards, then cross the Irish Sea to Scotland's noble fortresses. Kiss the Blarney Stone for the gift of eloquence, marvel at Giant's Causeway basalt columns, explore Edinburgh and Stirling's crown jewel castles. This is Celtic heritage at its most dramatic — emerald hills, volcanic crags, and ferry crossings between ancient kingdoms.

Begin in Cork's medieval heritage and journey north through Ireland's ancient heartland — Gothic fortresses, prehistoric coastlines and Dublin's literary soul. Private coaches connect each landmark across the emerald landscape.

  • Blarney Castle — kiss the Blarney Stone (1446 tower house)
  • Rock of Cashel — Celtic cathedral, Cormac's Chapel, round tower
  • Titanic Belfast — immersive museum where RMS Titanic was built
  • Giant's Causeway — Finn McCool's 40,000 hexagonal basalt columns
  • Trinity College Dublin — Book of Kells, Long Room library
  • Evening pub session with live traditional Irish music

A relaxed morning in Belfast before boarding the Stena Line Superferry across the Irish Sea — a scenic 2h25m crossing between two Celtic kingdoms.

  • Morning free in Belfast — City Hall, Peace Walls murals
  • St. George's Market — Victorian covered market, local produce
  • 2h25m Stena Line Superferry Belfast → Cairnryan (departs 15:30)
  • Private coach Cairnryan → Edinburgh (2h scenic drive)
  • Hotel check-in Edinburgh with welcome dinner
  • Evening stroll along Edinburgh Royal Mile

Edinburgh's volcanic castle rock launches your Scottish adventure — crown jewels, Renaissance palaces, and battlefields where kingdoms were forged. Day trip to Stirling reveals Scotland's most strategically important fortress.

  • Edinburgh Castle — crown jewels, Stone of Destiny, Mons Meg cannon
  • Royal Mile & Holyrood Palace — St. Giles Cathedral, Camera Obscura
  • Stirling Castle — James V Renaissance palace, Great Hall tapestries
  • Bannockburn Battlefield — Robert the Bruce 1314 victory site
  • Live ceilidh music farewell dinner in Edinburgh Old Town
  • Edinburgh Airport departure Day 11
